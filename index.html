Background

Washington DC, the capital of the United States, is a vibrant city filled with rich history, iconic landmarks, and cultural diversity.
Key sites include The White House, The National Mall, and The Lincoln Memorial, which symbolize American history and governance.

White House

The White House is the official residence and workplace of the President of the United States.
Located at 1600 Pennsylvania Avenue NW, it has been the residence of every U.S. president since John Adams in 1800.

National Mall

The National Mall is a national park in downtown Washington, D.C., featuring monuments, memorials, and museums.
It is the centerpiece of the city and hosts events such as presidential inaugurations and national celebrations.

Lincoln Memorial

The Lincoln Memorial honors the 16th President of the United States, Abraham Lincoln.
It features a large statue of Lincoln and inscriptions of his Gettysburg Address and Second Inaugural Address.
